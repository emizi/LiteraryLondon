Sherlock's London

Sherlock's London awaits you, step back in time to visit the landmarks in Sir Arthur Conan Doyle's Sherlock series, as well as places to commemorate the characters in modern day London.

Baker Street

  • 221B Baker Street
    The fictional address of Holmes and Watson.
  • Sherlock Holmes Museum
    The Sherlock Holmes museum recreates Victorian London with artefacts and treasures from the period.
  • Statue of Sherlock
    The statue of Sherlock Holmes in London, sculpted by John Doubleday, stands near the site of the fictional detective's home at 221B Baker Street. Unveiled on 23 September 1999, it was funded by the Abbey National building society, whose headquarters were on the supposed site of the famous address. As no site was available on Baker Street itself the statue is outside Baker Street tube station, on Marylebone Road. Doubleday had previously produced a statue of Holmes for the town of Meiringen in Switzerland, below the Reichenbach Falls from which the detective fell to his death in the story "The Final Problem".
  • Madame Tussauds
    Wax works of both Downey Jr and Cumberbatch as Sherlock reside here

Euston Square

  • Speedy's Cafe
    From the BBC tv show

Picadilly Circus

  • Cafe Royal
    A French restaurant where Holmes was attacked in 'The Adventure of the Illustrious Client.'
  • The Criterion Bar
    Where Watson first heard mention of Holmes, an eccentric fellow studying at St. Bartholomew's Hospital who needed a roommate.
  • Pall Mall
    The probable location of the Diogenes Club, Holmes's brother Mycroft's club, where members were forbidden to speak.

Oxford Circus

  • The Langham Hotel
    Hosted a dinner between Sir Arthur Conan Doyle and Oscar Wilde, which resulted in the creation of The Picture of Dorian Gray by Wilde and Holmes' second literary appearance in The Sign of Four by Conan Doyle. The hotel subsequently appears in later Sherlock mysteries.

Charing Cross

  • Charing Cross Hotel
    Here you can stand near the spot where Holmes caught a spy,opposite where Watson banked and kept his box of notes (probably Coutts Bank) and where, just across the street, they sent off urgent telegrams
  • Sherlock Holmes Pub
    The place where Holmes tracked down Francis Hay Moulton in The Noble Bachelors. Inside you'll find another replica of Holmes' study, as well as a huge amount of memorabilia, including Dr Watson's old service revolver and a collection of television and film stills.
  • The Lyceum Theatre
    Where meetings took place in The Sign of Four
  • Simpson's-in-the-Strand
    Sherlock Holmes' favourite restaurant
